## 1. EO GLOBAL STRUCTURE

### What is EO?
[Entrepreneurs' Organization](https://eonetwork.org/?scLang=en) is a global peer-to-peer community where successful entrepreneurs connect, learn, and grow together. It is designed to help business owners take their leadership and companies to the next level by leveraging peer relationships, shared experiences, and structured learning.

### Mission
Individual chapters, their members, and their activities are essential to implementing EO's mission and values at the local level and providing opportunities for peer-to-peer member engagement and growth.

### Scale
- **Members:** Nearly 20,000 peers globally
- **Chapters:** 220+ chapters
- **Countries:** 61 countries worldwide
- **Member Type:** Founders, owners, majority stakeholders of privately-held, revenue-generating businesses

### Chapter Structure
Local chapters are geographically based groups (typically 25–75 members) that run most EO activities. Chapter governance is volunteer-led, with chapters electing volunteer leaders and following EO governance guidelines. EO Global (headquarters) provides strategic direction, program development, brand management, and global event coordination.

## 3. EO FORUM — THE CORE EXPERIENCE

### What is EO Forum?
[EO Forum is a unique, confidential small peer group made up of 6-10 EO members from non-competitive industries, led by a trained peer Moderator.](https://eonetwork.org/membership/forum/?scLang=en) Forums meet monthly for deep peer learning and personal growth.

### Forum Structure & Frequency
- **Size:** Typically 6-10 members
- **Frequency:** Monthly meetings
- **Moderator:** A trained peer from within the Forum (elected annually, not a paid facilitator)
- **Duration:** Full-day or half-day meetings (typically 4-6 hours)
- **Confidentiality:** Completely confidential setting of trusted peers

### How Forum Works: Meeting Dynamics

#### 5% Reflections (Opening Segment)
[Members share their most significant moments and impact over the past month in business, personal, and family life.](https://eokillingit.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/07/5Reflections.pdf)

- **Duration:** 3-5 minutes per member
- **Preparation:** Members fill out 5% Reflection Sheets before the meeting
- **Focus:** Identify strongest feelings from the past month (in single words), and what caused those feelings (one sentence)
- **Preparation Time:** Members spend 30-60 minutes reviewing calendars and life events to highlight insights and self-discoveries
- **Purpose:** Enable vulnerability, prepared sharing; serve as the pivot point of the Forum meeting
- **Connection:** Forum mates listen to connect to their own resonant memories or struggles and share those vulnerably

#### Deep Dive / Issue Presentation
One Forum member presents an issue that is greatly affecting them—this issue could be business, personal, or family-related. The presenter:
- Provides background to their current situation
- Specifies the kind of assistance they are seeking
- Opens the floor for peer input (NOT advice)

#### Peer Input (Gestalt Language Protocol)
[Other Forum members respond using experience-sharing—not advice-giving. They share relevant first-hand experiences in a fact-based manner, offering what worked and didn't work.](https://eonetwork.org/blog/going-gestalt-in-your-business/)

### Confidentiality: The Iron-Clad Rule
[The confidentiality agreement is ironclad: "What's said in the room stays in the room."](https://eonyc.org/wp-content/uploads/EOResources/MeetingResources/ForumMeetingQuickReference.pdf)

- **Strict behavior codes** prohibit sharing any information outside of the private monthly meeting
- **Self-enforced:** The group itself enforces behavior codes
- **Consequence structure:** Each Forum establishes its own rules about confidentiality conduct and penalties if violated (i.e., removal from the Forum group)
- **Trust foundation:** This confidentiality creates psychological safety that enables vulnerability and honest sharing

### Forum Training Program (FTP)
[New Forum participants receive Forum Training Program—an immersive 8-hour workshop designed to equip members with tools and mindfulness essential to a Forum's success.](https://eonetwork.org/membership/forum/?scLang=en)

This ensures all Forum members understand:
- Confidentiality expectations
- Gestalt Language Protocol
- Meeting roles and responsibilities
- How to give and receive peer feedback
- The philosophy: "Share experience, not advice"

## 4. GESTALT PROTOCOL — THE CORE PHILOSOPHY

### What is Gestalt Language Protocol?
[Gestalt Language Protocol is the foundation of Forum interactions and the vehicle for personal growth in EO.](https://davidcummings.org/2015/04/09/gestalt-protocol/)

### Core Principle: Experience Over Advice
Rather than giving advice, [Forum members share relevant first-hand experiences in an effort to offer what worked and didn't work in a manner that's fact-based, as opposed to opinion-based.](https://eonetwork.org/blog/going-gestalt-in-your-business/)

**Key difference:**
- **Advice:** "You should do X because..." (prescriptive, one-size-fits-all)
- **Gestalt:** "When I faced a similar situation, I found Y worked because... and Z didn't work because..." (fact-based, experience-based)

### Why Gestalt Works
By speaking from experience instead of giving advice, the Gestalt mindset **teaches—it doesn't tell.** People can take their own learnings from others' experiences, resulting in greater and more long-term impact.

### Gestalt Practices in Forum
Key practices include:
- Asking clarifying questions (to deepen understanding, not to challenge)
- Sharing negative and positive experiences (balanced perspective)
- Writing down questions and thoughts (active listening)
- Paraphrasing and confirming what was said (ensure understanding)
- Avoiding prescriptive language ("you should...")
- Speaking in first-person ("when I...", "I found...")

### Real-World Impact Example
[One entrepreneur shared how this approach helped during a crisis: Using Gestalt proved effective, providing support during a difficult time in their business and the opportunity to communicate needs in a non-threatening environment.](https://www.eonetwork.org/octane-magazine/december-2011/how-gestalt-saved-my-business)

This creates a **tight-knit group of seven to ten EO members who meet monthly, offering genuine conversations in a confidential, ongoing, judgment-free environment.**

- Teaches how to manage Forum processes and members

### Key Moderator Responsibilities

- **Facilitation:** Lead the group through establishing the Forum vision and administration
- **Leadership example:** Lead by example in modeling confidentiality and vulnerability
- **Volunteer coordination:** Ask for volunteers for other roles (timekeeper, scribe, etc.)
- **Chapter engagement:** Participate in meetings with the chapter Forum Chair
- **Meeting management:** Critical Forum meeting functions with opportunities to practice Forum facilitation skills

## 9. EO CULTURE & LANGUAGE

### Core Philosophy: "Share Experience, Not Advice"
This is the differentiator of EO from other business groups like YPO, Vistage, or industry associations. EO prioritizes peer learning through lived experience, not expert prescriptions.

### Key EO Terminology
- **Forum:** The core 6-10 member peer group (meets monthly)
- **Moderator:** The elected peer leader of a Forum
- **Gestalt Language Protocol:** Experience-sharing, fact-based communication
- **5% Reflections:** Opening segment where members share monthly highlights/insights
- **Deep Dive:** In-depth exploration of one member's issue/challenge
- **Parking Lot:** Issues raised that go beyond current discussion (tabled for future agenda)
- **Confidentiality:** "What's said in the room stays in the room"
- **Peer-to-peer:** Member-to-member learning (not expert-facilitated)
- **All-Chapter Forum:** Mandatory monthly/quarterly gathering of entire chapter
- **Forum Trainer/Forum Leader:** Advanced role teaching Forum to new members

### How EO Differs from Other Business Groups

| Dimension | EO | YPO | Vistage |
|-----------|----|----|---------|
| **Core Method** | Peer experience sharing (Gestalt) | Peer learning + leadership coaching | Executive coaches + peer groups |
| **Facilitator** | Trained peer moderator (volunteer) | Dedicated chair/facilitator | Professional facilitator/coach |
| **Advice Model** | "Share experience, not advice" | Peer advice + coach guidance | Professional advice focus |
| **Confidentiality** | Ironclad peer covenant | Similar confidentiality | Similar confidentiality |
| **Growth Focus** | Personal + business + life integration | Leadership development + peer learning | Business metrics + execution |
| **Cost Structure** | Membership dues + local fees | Higher membership dues | Similar |
| **Member Profile** | $1M+ revenue owners | Typically $1M-$10M+ revenue | Typically $500K-$2M+ revenue |

### EO Culture & Values
- **Vulnerability:** Members share struggles, not just wins
- **Peer equality:** No hierarchy; all members have equal voice
- **Confidentiality as trust:** Safety enables honesty
- **Growth mindset:** Focus on becoming better leaders and humans
- **Non-judgment:** No "shoulds" or criticism; experience-based input
- **Action-oriented:** Members hold each other accountable to goals/commitments
