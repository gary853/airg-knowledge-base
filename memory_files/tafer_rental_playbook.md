## 6. Listing Optimization — Tips & Tricks

### Photography (Non-Negotiable)
- Professional high-res images ($300–$500 investment)
- Capture emotion: guests envisioning themselves there
- Show beach, pool, room, dining, sunset views
- Bright natural daylight, composition to maximize space feel
- Include lifestyle shots (couple on beach, family dining)

### Headline Formula
- Lead with best feature: "Beachfront All-Inclusive Cancun Luxury — Spring Break Paradise"
- 8–12 words max
- Include location + season appeal

### Description Strategy
- Tell a story, not a feature list
- Stack amenities strategically (meals, drinks, beach, WiFi, family-friendly)
- Trust indicators: "Verified owner," "100% satisfaction"
- Be transparent about any limitations

### Pricing Psychology
- Price 5–10% below comparable listings → drives inquiry volume
- Peak season: List high ($600), wait 7 days, drop to $500 (creates urgency)
- Offer length-of-stay discounts: 7+ nights = 5% off, 14+ = 10% off
- Never list as "Contact for Price" — kills inquiry rates
- Refresh listing every 30 days to stay top of search

## 7. Guest Certificate & Logistics

### How Guest Certificates Work
1. Book your week/nights using your TAFER points via ResortCom portal
2. Request guest certificate from TAFER member services (name of guest required)
3. Provide guest with: printable confirmation + guest certificate + check-in instructions
4. Guest presents certificate + valid ID + major credit card at check-in (for incidentals)
5. RedWeek verifies guest name before finalization if using their platform

### Payment Handling
- **Recommended:** Credit card via Stripe/PayPal, or platform escrow (RedWeek/KOALA)
- **Acceptable:** Zelle (peer transfers)
- **AVOID:** Wire transfers, money orders (irreversible, high fraud risk)
- **Security deposit:** 25–50% of rental cost, charged at booking, refunded post-stay
- **International guests:** Wise, PayPal, or Stripe (cross-border support)

### Written Rental Agreement (Required for Every Booking)
Include: dates, total amount, guest name, house rules, cancellation policy, damage responsibility, check-in/out times (typically 4 PM / 10 AM), all-inclusive meal plan enrollment instructions.

### Cancellation Terms (Recommended)
- 60+ days before check-in: Full refund
- 30–60 days: 50% refund
- Less than 30 days: Forfeit deposit

## 8. Tax Obligations (Critical)

### Mexico
- **25% tax** on gross rental income (non-resident foreign owners)
- **16% IVA** (Value Added Tax) on furnished units
- Must register for Mexican tax ID (RFC) if receiving rental income
- Rental income must be declared to Mexican tax authorities

### United States
- Report rental income on US tax return
- FBAR filing may apply if funds held in Mexican accounts
- Deductible expenses: maintenance fees, platform fees, travel for property management
- Consult Kevin Wright (Capstone CPA) for integration with AIRG tax strategy

## 9. Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Overpricing:** Listings 20%+ above median sit unbooked. Better to get 80% occupancy at fair price.
2. **Poor photography:** Blurry/dark photos tank inquiry rates. Invest in professional shots.
3. **Vague descriptions:** "Nice condo" = zero conversions. "Beachfront all-inclusive with complimentary drinks" = 3x better.
4. **Forgetting contract restrictions:** RCI/II exchanges CANNOT be rented. Violation = lose points + income.
5. **Undersetting baseline:** Don't just aim to break even on $525 MF. Peak season can net $4K–$6K.
6. **Unreliable payment methods:** Wire transfers = theft risk. Use escrow or verified platforms only.
7. **No guest vetting:** Always ask purpose of stay, guest count. Red flags = "surprise guests" or over-capacity.
8. **Ignoring hurricane season:** Don't offer cheap Aug–Oct rates without cancellation buffer. Close bookings Aug 15–Nov 15.
9. **Renting off-platform:** Craigslist/Facebook = high fraud risk. Use RedWeek/KOALA/TUG escrow.
10. **Inflexible cancellation:** Strict no-refund = fewer bookings. Offer 30–60 day flexibility.

## 12. Quick Decision Framework

**"Should I rent or use this week?"**
1. Is it peak season (Dec/Mar/Jul)? → **Rent** (unless you specifically want that week)
2. Can you get $300+/night? → **Rent**
3. Is it hurricane season (Aug–Oct)? → **Neither** (don't risk guest cancellations)
4. Is it a personal vacation you've planned? → **Use** (the $0.13/point value is already incredible)
5. Will points expire unused? → **Rent at any price** (recovering even $200 beats losing points)
