### Break-In Requirements (Per Lycoming SI 1427C)

#### Ground Operations (Before First Flight)
- Pre-oil engine (prime oil system per SI 1241)
- Initial run at 1,000 RPM until oil temp reaches ~140°F, then 1,500 RPM for 15 minutes
- Monitor: oil pressure, oil temp, CHT
- Full static power brief run (up to 10 seconds)
- Return to idle and check idle mixture
- Shut down and inspect for leaks
- Remove oil screen/filter and inspect for contamination
- If clean → proceed to flight test

#### First Flight Break-In Procedure
- **Duration:** 2 hours minimum
- **Power Setting:** 65–75% continuous (mandatory, not optional)
- **Altitude:** Below 5,000 ft density altitude (to achieve proper power)
- **Mixture:** Full rich (do NOT lean during first break-in flight)
- **Hour 1:** Continuous 75% power
  - Example power setting: 24" MP @ 2,400 RPM (exact setting per PA-28-235 POH)
- **Hour 2:** Alternate every ~45 minutes between 75% and 65% power
  - 75% ≈ 24" MP @ 2,400 RPM
  - 65% ≈ 22" MP @ 2,400 RPM
- **Descent:** AVOID idle descents (risk of ring flutter and cylinder damage); use controlled descent with power

#### Critical CHT & Oil Parameters
- **CHT Target:** 350–400°F (do NOT exceed 420°F)
- **Oil Temperature:** 180–200°F
- **Oil Pressure:** Within operator manual limits
- **Oil Consumption Formula:** Max allowable for 235 HP O-540:
  - 0.006 × 235 × 4 ÷ 7.4 ≈ 0.76 qt/hr (upper limit)
  - Expect trending lower over first 10–15 hours as rings seat

#### Oil & Maintenance Post-Break-In
- Continue mineral oil (Aeroshell 100) until oil consumption stabilizes
- Then transition to ashless dispersant oil
- Oil change schedule: Monitor closely during first 10–15 hours
- Oil screen inspection: Daily for first week, then normal schedule
