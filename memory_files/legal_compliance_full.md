### B. Core Defense Strategy

**Primary Position: AIRG Is a Property Manager, Not an Employer**

All Inclusive Realty Group Inc. operates strictly as a **licensed property manager** acting under written Property Management Agreements (PMAs) with property owners. AIRG does **not employ** maintenance vendors, cleaning contractors, or repair specialists. Instead:

- AIRG coordinates independent third-party contractors with licenses, invoices, and separate business operations
- These contractors operate on their own authority under separate agreements with property owners or AIRG as agent
- AIRG's role: rent collection, tenant communication, repair coordination, fund remittance—**agent duties, not employment duties**

**Key Defense Sub-Points (For Hearing):**

1. **Property Management Agreement Structure**
   - AIRG acts as licensed agent for property owners under formal written PMAs
   - Separation of ownership (property owners) from management (AIRG)
   - Legitimizes AIRG as intermediary, not employer of field workers

2. **Worker Classification Under ABC Test** (Cal. Labor Code §2775-2787; AB 5)
   - **A-Prong (Control):** Independent contractors operate under their own business names, carry licenses, invoice AIRG/owners, control their work methods
   - **B-Prong (Usual Business):** Maintenance, cleaning, repairs operate outside AIRG's core real estate brokerage/PM functions
   - **C-Prong (Independent Trade):** Contractors are established in their own trades (plumbing, HVAC, cleaning, etc.) and serve other clients
   - Documentation exists: contractor agreements, invoices, vendor licenses, payment records, Propertyware work order links

3. **Owner Compensation Structure** (Not Subject to Payroll Tax)
   - Gary Burmaster has **not received payroll wages** classified as employee compensation under CUIC §621(a), §926
   - All compensation is via **owner's draws/distributions** of profit (not wages)
   - Owner draws are **not subject to EDD payroll taxation** — they do not constitute "remuneration for services performed by an employee"
   - IRS filings and CPA documentation confirm this treatment; owner compensation is properly reported on personal tax returns, not as corporate payroll

4. **Good Faith Compliance**
   - AIRG cooperated fully with EDD audit process
   - Responded to all EDD inquiries and requests on time
   - No evidence of willful evasion or bad faith classification
   - Untimely petition filing was due to EDD audit staff turnover + miscommunication about assessment timelines

5. **Penalty Abatement Request**
   - Circumstances warrant mitigation (good faith, procedural delays, etc.)
   - Request penalty abatement under California law provisions
