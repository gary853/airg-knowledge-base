## QUICK REFERENCE — MOST-USED CLAUSES

### PMA (Property Management Agreement)
| Situation | Clause | Accountability | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Owner wants to terminate | **Termination Clause** | Owner must provide written notice per contract term (typically 30 days) | Check PMA for exact notice window |
| AIRG need to collect unpaid rent | **Fee Authority** | Owner authorizes AIRG to collect management fees from rent collected | Management fee = % of rent (current: 8-10%) |
| Owner refuses maintenance | **Maintenance Authority** | AIRG authorized up to $250 for non-emergency repairs; over $250 requires written owner approval | Failure to approve = owner liable for damage escalation |
| Tenant damage claim vs owner | **Trust Account / Security Deposit** | AIRG holds deposits per CA law; itemized deductions only; balance due within 21 days of move-out | Protect with photo documentation & vendor invoices |

### Vendor Agreement
| Situation | Clause | Accountability | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Vendor late on job | **Performance Standards / NTE** | Vendor must complete within agreed timeline; delays incur rental loss penalties (tied to daily rent) | Preferred vendor = Net 15-30 payment terms |
| Vendor quotes $300, invoices $400 | **NTE (Not to Exceed)** | Any variance >10% requires written approval before work proceeds; overbilling = payment held pending audit | Preferred vendor fee = 15% markup on materials |
| Vendor won't provide insurance cert | **Insurance Requirements** | Vendor must maintain GL coverage; name AIRG as additional insured; supply updated cert before work starts | No work order approval without current cert on file |
| Vendor missing documentation | **Invoice Requirements** | Invoices must include: WO#, invoice date, description, amount, vendor sign-off. Submitted within 48 hours of completion. | Missing invoices = payment delayed until complete |

### Lease Agreement (Tenant)
| Situation | Clause | Accountability | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Rent due, tenant pays late | **Late Fee Clause** | 10% of rent = late fee (if stated in lease); charged month-to-month; NSF $25 first / $35 thereafter | Fee must be *itemized* on statement; send reminder notice |
| Tenant owes security deposit refund | **Security Deposit Clause** | Deposit held per CA law; itemized deductions for damage only; balance due within 21 days of move-out | Damage beyond normal wear = cost deducted; rest refunded |
| Tenant damaged unit, won't pay | **Default/Eviction Clause** | Non-payment >5 days = notice to pay or quit (3-day notice); breach of lease terms = similar notice | **CRITICAL:** Cannot charge tenant for *serving* notice. Costs = owner responsibility. |
| Tenant has unauthorized pet | **Pet Clause** | "No pets without written consent/addendum." Breach = default; can charge pet rent per lease. | Must be in original lease or authorized addendum |

## Overview

## 1.1 Fee Structure

### Management Fee
- **Rate:** 8-10% of gross rent collected (confirm exact % per owner contract)
- **Trigger:** Collected from rent; AIRG retains % before owner statement
- **When due:** Monthly, upon rent collection
- **Owner statement:** Provided on 10th, 15th, 20th, 25th of month

### Leasing Fee
- **When charged:** New tenant placement
- **Amount:** Typically 50-100% of first month's rent (confirm per contract)
- **Paid by:** Owner
- **What's included:** Advertising, tenant screening, lease preparation, move-in coordination

### Renewal Fee
- **When charged:** Tenant lease renewal (extension, not new lease)
- **Amount:** Typically 50% of leasing fee (confirm per contract)
- **Paid by:** Owner
- **Trigger:** When renewal agreement is executed

### Maintenance Coordination Fee
- **When charged:** Per work order issued to vendor
- **Amount:** Typically $25-50 per WO (confirm per contract)
- **Paid by:** Owner (deducted from owner statement or invoiced separately)
- **What's included:** Coordinating vendor dispatch, inspections, photo documentation, invoice processing

### Inspection Fee
- **When charged:** Move-in, move-out, or periodic property inspections (zInspector)
- **Amount:** Per inspection (confirm rate — typically $50-150)
- **Paid by:** Owner
- **What's included:** Full photographic report, condition assessment, damage documentation

### Late Fee Split (Tenant Late Rent)
- **Owner's standard:** 10% of rent if tenant pays late
- **AIRG retention:** % of late fee (confirm split per PMA — typically 50% to AIRG, 50% to owner)
- **Trigger:** Tenant payment received after due date
- **Limit:** CA law prohibits unreasonable fees; 10% is generally acceptable

# SECTION 3: RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

## Overview
**What it is:** Contract between AIRG (as PM/lease issuer) and tenant.  
**Who signs:** Tenant(s) + AIRG rep (Gary Burmaster, DRE #01408894) on behalf of owner.  
**Form:** California Association of Realtors (CAR) Residential Lease or custom template based on CAR form.  
**Current template:** AIRG Standard Residential Lease (2025) — based on CAR form with AIRG customizations.

---

## 3.1 Rent Terms

### Rent Amount & Due Date
- **Monthly rent:** Fixed per lease (e.g., $1,950/month for 2361 Thompson Way)
- **Due date:** Specified day each month (typically 1st or 5th; must be consistent)
- **Payment method:** Tenant portal (primary); in-person payment during office hours (Mon-Fri 8am-5pm); or mail to AIRG address
- **Post office rule:** If due date falls on weekend/holiday, payment due next business day

### Late Fees
- **Rate:** 10% of monthly rent (standard CA lease provision)
- **When triggered:** If rent not received by end of due date (e.g., due 5th, late charge applies 6th)
- **Frequency:** Late fee charged each month rent is late (not capped)
- **NSF (Non-Sufficient Funds):** $25 first occurrence / $35 second + subsequent

**Example:**
- Rent: $1,950/month
- Late fee: $195 (10% of $1,950)
- If tenant pays late 2 months: $1,950 × 2 + ($195 × 2) + NSF fee if check bounced

**CA Law compliance:** 10% late fee is reasonable and enforceable (not considered "penalty").

---

## 3.2 Security Deposit & Move-In Funds

### Deposit Amount
- **Standard:** Equal to 1 month's rent (CA law max = 1 month for unfurnished; 1.5 months for furnished)
- **Held by:** Owner or AIRG acting as agent (must be disclosed on lease)
- **Trust account:** Deposited in AIRG trust account (separate, not commingled)
- **Interest:** Generally not paid to tenant (unless CA city ordinance requires)

### Move-In Funds
- **Total due at signing:** Usually rent + deposit + prorated utilities/fees
- **Example (2361 Thompson Way):**
  - Security deposit: $3,900
  - First month rent: $1,950
  - Prorated rent (mid-month): $975
  - Utilities deposit (water): $175
  - **Total due: $6,050**

### Deposit Refund (Move-Out)
- **Timeline:** Within 21 days of move-out, AIRG must return deposit or provide itemized deduction list
- **Deductions allowed:** Only actual damages beyond normal wear and tear (CA law strict)
- **Itemized statement:** Must show: damage description, vendor quote/invoice, amount deducted, net refund
- **Remaining balance:** Refunded by check to tenant within 21 days
- **Dispute process:** If tenant disagrees, AIRG provides cost documentation

**Example (move-out scenario):**
- Original deposit: $3,900
- Carpet stain repair: $300
- Paint touch-up: $150
- Cleaning (if required): $200
- **Total deductions: $650**
- **Refund due: $3,250** (within 21 days)

## 3.3 Tenant Maintenance Responsibilities

### What Tenant Must Do
- **Keep premises clean:** Free of trash, pests, excessive clutter
- **Yard/landscaping:** Mow lawn if required; clear weeds if listed on move-in inspection
- **Appliances:** Use properly; don't abuse (normal wear accepted)
- **Utilities:** Keep water running in winter (prevent freeze); report leaks immediately
- **Pest control:** Report pest activity; cooperate with vendor access
- **Smoking/cannabis:** Strictly prohibited (lease states "no smoking of any substance")

### What Tenant Cannot Do
- **Structural changes:** No painting, wallpaper, nail holes (minor pin holes OK)
- **Locks/security:** Cannot change locks without landlord permission
- **Alterations:** No major renovations without written approval
- **Subletting:** Cannot rent to others without written lease amendment

### Notification Requirements
- **Maintenance request:** Tenant submits via tenant portal or phone
- **Response timeline:** AIRG acknowledges within 1 business day; vendor scheduled within 2-3 days (non-emergency)
- **Emergency (habitability):** Tenant must report immediately (HVAC failure in heat, water loss, electrical hazard, pest infestation)

---

## 3.4 Landlord (Owner/AIRG) Maintenance Responsibilities

### What Owner Must Provide
- **Habitability:** Functional heating, water, sewage, electrical, weatherproofing (CA Civil Code §1941)
- **Safety:** Working locks, secure windows, safe entry/egress, pest-free at move-in
- **Appliances (if provided):** Must be in working order at move-in; repairs landlord responsibility
- **Access to repairs:** Landlord must allow vendor access with 24-hour notice (except emergencies)

### What Owner Is NOT Responsible For
- **Tenant-caused damage:** Broken windows from tenant accident, holes from tenant nails
- **Normal wear:** Faded paint, worn carpet (unless unreasonable), minor scuffs
- **Lack of air conditioning:** AC is not legally required in CA (unless lease specifically includes it)

### Emergency vs. Non-Emergency Response
- **Emergency (same day):** HVAC failure in 110°F heat, no water, electrical hazard, broken lock
- **Non-emergency (3-5 days):** Dripping faucet, paint touch-up, appliance malfunction (if not critical)
- **Owner refusal:** If owner refuses to make repairs, tenant can pay + deduct from rent (CA law) — escalates liability

## 3.5 Lease Violations & Remedies

### Non-Payment of Rent
- **Timeline:** Rent due on specified date; late if not received by end of day
- **Notice required:** Send reminder notice to tenant (courtesy, not required)
- **3-day notice trigger:** If rent still unpaid after 5-7 days, AIRG can issue 3-day notice to pay or quit
- **Legal process:** 3-day notice starts eviction clock; if unpaid after 3 days, attorney files unlawful detainer

**CRITICAL:** Cannot charge tenant for *serving* notice (CA law violation). Service costs paid by owner.

### Unauthorized Occupants/Pets
- **Lease clause:** "No pets without written consent/addendum"; no unauthorized occupants
- **Detection:** Usually discovered at renewal or tenant complaint
- **Remedy:** Issue notice to cure or quit (typically 3 days)
- **Pet rent:** If unauthorized pet discovered, tenant can be charged pet rent retroactively if lease allows

**Example:**
- Lease: "No pets without approval"
- Tenant gets dog: Breach of lease
- AIRG response: "You have 3 days to remove dog or we proceed with eviction"
- Alternative: Sign pet addendum + charge pet rent going forward ($25-50/month typical)

### Lease Property Damage Beyond Normal Wear
- **Definition:** Holes in walls beyond picture hangers, stains/damage to carpet, broken appliances from misuse
- **Notice:** Inspect at move-out; document with photos
- **Remedy:** Itemize damages; deduct from security deposit (landlord keeps funds)
- **Owner responsibility:** If damage escalates (water damage), owner may pursue additional damages beyond deposit

### Smoking/Cannabis Violations
- **Lease clause:** "No smoking of any substance on premises"
- **Detection:** Odor, visible ash, tenant report
- **Remedy:** 3-day notice to cure (stop smoking) or quit (vacate)
- **Enforcement:** If tenant continues, proceed with eviction

### Other Lease Breaches (Noise, Guests, etc.)
- **Typically:** 3-day cure or quit notice
- **Follow-up:** If tenant ignores notice, attorney initiates unlawful detainer

## 3.6 Notice Procedures (CRITICAL COMPLIANCE ISSUE)

### 3-Day Notice (Pay or Quit)
- **Use when:** Rent unpaid after 5+ days
- **Content:** "Dear [Tenant], rent for [month] is past due in the amount of $[amount]. You have 3 days to pay in full or vacate."
- **Service:** Must be personally delivered, posted on door, AND mailed (per CA CCP §1161)
- **Cost:** Attorney drafts ($100-200); service fee ($50-75); postage ($1)
- **WHO PAYS:** Owner must pay — cannot charge tenant (CA law violation)

### 30-Day Notice (Lease Termination)
- **Use when:** Month-to-month lease; owner wants tenant to leave; month-to-month only
- **Content:** "Tenant must vacate by [date 30 days out]"
- **Service:** Same as 3-day (personal, posted, mailed)
- **Cost:** Minimal (property manager or attorney; postage)
- **WHO PAYS:** Owner pays

### 60-Day Notice (Lease Termination with Cause)
- **Use when:** Local rent control (e.g., Sacramento, Folsom); "just cause" requirement
- **Content:** Specifies reason (non-payment, lease breach, end of tenancy, etc.)
- **Service:** Personal, posted, mailed
- **Cost:** Property manager + postage
- **WHO PAYS:** Owner pays

### Move-Out Inspection Notice
- **Timing:** 48 hours before move-out (CA law requires notice)
- **Content:** "We will conduct move-out inspection on [date] at [time]. Tenant must be present."
- **Documentation:** Inspection photos + condition report (zInspector)

## 3.7 Eviction Process Timeline

**Note:** Full eviction typically takes 30-60 days after 3-day notice expires.

1. **Day 0:** Rent unpaid; AIRG sends reminder notice
2. **Day 5:** If still unpaid, attorney issues 3-day notice (served personally + posted + mailed)
3. **Day 8:** 3-day period expires; if unpaid, attorney files Unlawful Detainer
4. **Day 10-15:** Sheriff serves defendant (tenant); response due in 5 days
5. **Day 20-30:** Trial/judgment; judge rules
6. **Day 30-45:** If landlord wins, sheriff enforces eviction (locks out tenant, removes belongings)

**Cost to owner:** $1,000-2,000+ total (attorney, court, sheriff)

**Accountability:** Joshua Clark (eviction attorney) handles; Gary or Kane approves before filing.

## 3.8 Move-Out & Deposit Handling

### Move-Out Inspection
- **Scheduling:** Notify tenant 48 hours before move-out
- **Documentation:** Photos of every room, closets, appliances, exterior
- **Comparison:** Match move-in photos to move-out photos (zInspector side-by-side)
- **Damage assessment:** Identify damage beyond normal wear immediately

### Itemized Deduction List
- **Due to tenant:** Within 21 days of move-out
- **Required content:**
  - Property address
  - Lease dates
  - Damage description (per room)
  - Vendor quote/invoice (must be actual bid, not assumed)
  - Amount deducted
  - Refund amount (if any)
  - Method of refund (check, ACH)
- **Example damage costs (CA market):**
  - Carpet cleaning/repair: $100-300 per room

- **What Brenda does:** If tenant reports smoking smell, document (photos, odor intensity)

- **Remedy:** Issue 3-day notice to cure or quit

- **What Brenda/Alex does:** During inspections or tenant contacts, verify who lives in unit
- **Red flag:** "My cousin is staying 3 weeks"; if extends, becomes occupant = breach
- **Remedy:** Remind tenant of lease; if continues, 3-day notice
