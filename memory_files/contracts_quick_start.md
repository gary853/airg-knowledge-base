## Search Strategy (How to Find What You Need)

**If you have a question like...**

### "Can I approve this work order?"
1. Search for: **$250** (in Vendor or PMA section)
2. Answer: If under $250 + non-emergency → Yes. If over $250 → Get written owner approval first.

### "Can we charge the tenant for this?"
1. Search for: **"charge tenant"** or **"can we"**
2. Common answers: 
   - Can charge for late rent? **Yes (10% late fee)**
   - Can charge for notice service? **No (CA law violation)**
   - Can charge for unauthorized pet? **Yes (if lease allows)**

### "What if vendor is late?"
1. Search for: **"delay"** or **"timeline"**
2. Answer: Document + escalate to Kane after 2nd incident. Performance-based penalties still under review.

### "How do we handle the deposit?"
1. Search for: **"deposit"** or **"21 days"**
2. Answer: Itemize deductions, refund remaining balance within 21 days. Miss deadline = legal liability.

### "When must something happen?"
1. Search for time-based keywords: **"48 hours"**, **"5 days"**, **"21 days"**, **"30 days"**
2. Examples:
   - Vendor invoice: 48 hours
   - Lease termination notice: 30 days
   - Deposit refund: 21 days
   - Non-emergency maintenance: 3-5 days

## Common Scenarios You'll Face (With Answers)

### Scenario 1: Vendor Quotes $250, Invoices $300
**Question:** Can we pay the $300?  
**Answer:** No. 10% over NTE = hold payment. Send to Gary for approval.  
**Reference:** Vendor 2.4 ("Overbilling remedy")  
**Lesson:** Always get written NTE before work starts.

### Scenario 2: Tenant Won't Move Furniture to Paint Room
**Question:** Can we charge tenant for moving labor?  
**Answer:** No. Tenant is responsible for clearing their own space. If tenant refuses, document + escalate to Gary.  
**Reference:** Lease 3.3 ("What Tenant Must Do")  
**Lesson:** Lease says "keep premises" — that includes making space available.

### Scenario 3: Tenant Moves Out; Deposit is $4,000. Damage is $600.
**Question:** When must we refund $3,400?  
**Answer:** Within 21 days of move-out, with itemized deduction list. Must include: damage description, vendor quote/invoice, amount deducted.  
**Reference:** Lease 3.8 ("Deposit Refund")  
**Lesson:** Miss 21-day deadline = tenant sues for full $4,000 + statutory damages.

### Scenario 4: Owner Calls at 3 PM; Tenant Has No Water
**Question:** Can AIRG approve emergency plumber without owner approval?  
**Answer:** Yes. This is habitability emergency. Approve same-day. Notify owner immediately. Send invoice to owner statement.  
**Reference:** PMA 1.2 ("Emergency vs. Non-Emergency")  
**Lesson:** Tenant safety > approval delays. Document everything.

### Scenario 5: Vendor Has Not Invoiced After 10 Days
**Question:** Should we contact them?  
**Answer:** Yes. By day 7, contact vendor: "Invoice not received. Due within 48 hours per agreement. Send to accounting@airg with photos."  
**Reference:** Vendor 2.3 ("Invoice Requirements")  
**Lesson:** Late invoices delay payment cycles. Follow up proactively.
