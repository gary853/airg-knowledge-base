- **Lowest recorded:** 188.2 lbs (Feb 2026)

- **Net change from peak:** –42 lbs (~18% bodyweight reduction)
- **Goal:** 175 lbs (BMI ~25.8)

### Phase Timeline

**Phase 1 — Initial Ozempic Ramp (Sep–Oct 2024): 208 → 195 lbs**
- Strong early responder. Appetite suppression clearly effective.
- Dose escalation aggressive but tolerated (0.125 → 1.0 mg over 10 weeks).
- Cleanest momentum window. Body responds quickly when dosing + cadence are stable.

**Phase 2 — Plateau & Muscle Noise (Nov 2024): 194 → 196 lbs**
- Weight rebound attributed to resistance training (likely recomposition, not fat regain).
- Scale-only tracking began muddying the signal.

**Phase 3 — Interruptions & Rebuild (Dec 2024–May 2025): 202 → ~200 lbs**
- Weeks off medication = predictable rebound. Restart cycles caused metabolic whiplash.
- Dose jumping (0.4 → 1.1 → 0.5) reduced consistency.
- Stops and starts cost ~6 months of efficiency.

**Phase 4 — Stabilization (Jun–Jul 2025): 199 → 194 lbs**
- Clean, steady decline. Proof that stable cadence = weight follows.

**Phase 5 — Tirzepatide Era (Oct 2025–Feb 2026): 197 → 188–191 lbs**
- Slower but more durable loss than Ozempic.
- Vacation spike (+5 lbs) corrected quickly.
- Lowest weight achieved here, not on Ozempic.
- Tirzepatide appears more sustainable long-term.

## 11. Automation Opportunities

### Garmin Connect → AI Health Dashboard (Priority 1)
**Goal:** Automated weekly/monthly health reports pulled from Garmin data
**Architecture Options:**

**Option A — Python garminconnect library (Recommended)**
- Library: `garminconnect` (PyPI) — Python3 API wrapper for Garmin Connect
- Capabilities: HR, sleep, stress, body composition, SpO2, blood pressure, steps
- Run on Machine 2 (Reid's Mac Mini) as scheduled task
- Output: JSON → structured health report → post to Teams or email to Gary
- GitHub: github.com/cyberjunky/python-garminconnect

**Option B — GarminDB (SQLite database approach)**
- Extracts sleep, weight, resting HR from Garmin Connect → stores as JSON/SQLite
- Good for historical analysis and Jupyter notebook dashboards
- GitHub: github.com/tcgoetz/GarminDB

**Option C — GarminGo (CSV/Sheets export)**
- Pulls daily health metrics → saves to CSV files
- Updated March 2026 with major enhancements
- GitHub: github.com/jagbanana/jg-garmin-to-sheets

### Proposed Pipeline
1. **Daily pull:** garminconnect library fetches previous day's data (weight, BP, HR, sleep, steps, stress)
2. **Weekly summary:** Aggregate 7-day averages, flag anomalies (BP spike, weight gain, poor sleep)
3. **Monthly dashboard:** Full health snapshot with trend charts, scoring, medication adherence
4. **Delivery:** Post to Teams channel or email — formatted health brief
5. **Integration:** Feed into CEO Daily Briefing (health section)

### Implementation Path
- **Script location (planned):** `/Users/airgmacmini2/AIRG/sync/garmin_health_sync.py`
- **Schedule:** Daily at 6:00 AM on Machine 2
- **Dependencies:** garminconnect library, Gary's Garmin Connect credentials (store in env vars)
- **Output:** `/Users/airgmacmini2/AIRG/memory/health/` (daily JSON + weekly/monthly MD summaries)
- **Alert triggers:** BP > 140/90, weight gain > 3 lbs in 7 days, resting HR > 75, sleep < 5 hrs
